After working on corporate blog and social promotion campaigns for Appcelerator, Cisco, Google, McAfee, SAP, and VMware, I developed a list of best practices to drive traffic to B2B technology blogs.  These practices were developed by working with large, global tech brands.

The results are listed by ROI, best to least effective.
  1. SEO;
  2. Cross-promotion with other channels such as Facebook;
  3. Social media advertising on Twitter, LinkedIn;
  4. Physical event interaction such as taking pictures and videos at a trade show and putting them up on the corporate blog or onsite Twitter reporting;
  5. Other campaigns such as surveys, contests, chats.
The results are definitely impacted by the existing campaigns, which are constantly running at large, global companies.  A campaign like SEO is also reliant upon content generation.  Marketing and developer relations campaigns are now very interconnected.  

To zoom in on the results of content generation and community interaction for software developers, I decided to run some experiments on a smaller scale for Python developer blogs.   I created a blog to teach children to program in Python and analyzed the referral traffic for six months. 

In order to control my experiment results, I limited my promotional activities to online interaction with other developers.  I also focused on Python developers with a very narrow scope.  I am only targeting parents that are teaching their children to program in Python.  The blog and videos are averaging 4,000 views a month, which is a reasonable set of data, especially considering the very narrow scope of the Python for children (PyChildren) topic.

For this experiment, the top referring URLs in order to effectiveness are:
  1. Stackoverflow question and answer about using Android Debugger on Linux and finding the vendor ID of the Rockwell RK3066 chipset used in Android tablets.  A complete stranger posted a link to my blog as the answer to a Stackoverflow question.  This is the largest source of traffic by far. 
  2. Other Python blogs where I posted a question or an answer that linked back to my blog.
  3. Forum on Pygame Subset for Android development. (I posted the answer and link)
  4. YouTube video on creating JSON mapfiles with Tiled (I created the video)
  5. YouTube video on 2D sprite animation (I created the video)
The top search keyword is the name of my blog pychildren.blogspot.com.    The other keywords are deal with using the library, pygame, that I describe how to use.

Top keywords for organic search in order of effectiveness are:
  1. pygame alternatives
  2. pygame and android
  3. pygame gamepad
  4. pygame examples
From this data, I drew the following conclusions:
  1. Stackoverflow is in a category by itself.  It was the most effective.
  2. Other community blogs targeting developers
  3. Forums targeting developers
  4. Most viewers are looking for information on how to use the pygame library or they are making a decision on whether to use the pygame library or an alternative.
From my previous experience with a full set of services, I know that targeting developers usually involves a mix of advertising, public relations, and social media.
